DMV Interactive Form


Designing a new interactive web based experience for the standard Vehicle Registration application

Tools: Figma and Illustrator

Overview


The DMV isn’t everyone’s favorite place, the goal of this project was to change that. I was to design a new interactive web based experience for the standard Vehicle Registration application, starting off with the DMV’s MV-82 form.


Problems


The DMV's current paper form is very outdated, it's riddled with many problems obvious at first glance.

Outdated - As most printed forms do, the design feels very outdated. The use of blue, grey, and back for separation causes a strain on viewers.

Grouping / White Space - Though there is an attempt to chunk the elements together, the groups are packed very tightly together causing the form to be difficult to read. The form doesn't even provide enough space to actually write in information on some fields.

Complex - The form is full of automotive jargon that the design assumes users are familiar with to be able to answer in depth questions. The form fails when users aren't able to answer with important information correctly.


Solutions


Switching form a paper to web format allows for the ability to bring ease to the user and focus to the design

Fresh Design - A web based format allowed me to make the experience of completing a form more enjoyable through interaction, type, color, human centered wording and graphics, shattering the DMV stereotype of being a place where souls go cold. Improved design was approachable and accessible for the full range of possible users, from a teenager to the elderly.

Leveraging Data - Codes such as the VIN number and license ID that contained much of the data that the user is being asked to provide, why make users do more work when the technology is there.

Hiding / Revealing Information - The design cleaned up by utilizing auto-generating fields, revealing information as needed, helping the viewer to feel less overwhelmed and in control.
